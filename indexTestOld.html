Workouts


Aerobic:

Aerobic exercise is exercise that speeds up your heartrate and breathing. There are many benefits of aerobic exercise. These benefits include increasing endurance, burning body fat, boosting your mood, and reducing your risk for several health issues such as type 2 diabetes, depression, falls, and heart disease and more. It is recommended that you spend 150 minutes a week doing moderate-intensity activities such as swimming, cycling, jogging, brisk walking, jogging and more. If you have limited space, you can try marching in place.


Stretching:

You should stretch before exercise and at least 3-4 times a week to loosen up muscles, for your own safety. Start with dynamic stretches then move onto static stretches.

Strength training:

You should do this exercise for the health benefits beyond simply strength such as improved bone growth. Do it 2-3 times a week. body weight exercises, like pushups are a good place to start.

Balance exercises:

Young and healthy people tend to leave balance exercises to seniors, people that are old. What they might forget is that training your balances can help or prevent a lot of muscle injuries. Yoga or Tai Chi are among the most popular balance workouts. It is important to mention that balance exercises are very effective when it comes to physical therapy because it helps to strengthen the muscles, joint flexibility and much more.
